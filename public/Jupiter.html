Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a diameter of approximately 86,881 miles (139,822 kilometers) at its equator. It is the fifth planet from the Sun and is located between Mars and Saturn. Jupiter is primarily composed of gas and has no solid surface, with a thick atmosphere of hydrogen, helium, and other gases. The planet's atmosphere features colorful bands of clouds and a massive, swirling storm known as the Great Red Spot. Jupiter has at least 79 known moons, including the four largest, known as the Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. These moons are some of the largest objects in the solar system, with Ganymede being the largest moon in the solar system. Jupiter's strong magnetic field creates intense radiation belts around the planet, making it a challenging environment for spacecraft to navigate. Despite this, several spacecraft have visited Jupiter, including the Galileo spacecraft and the Juno mission, which is currently in orbit around the planet. Jupiter is also known for its role in protecting the inner planets, including Earth, from asteroids and comets. Its gravitational pull helps to deflect many of these objects away from the inner solar system.
